Patient: Sarah M., 45F
Date: 2025-03-05

Chief Complaint: Persistent heartburn and acid regurgitation

History of Present Illness:
Mrs. M is a 45-year-old female presenting with a 3-month history of heartburn occurring 4-5 times per week. She reports acid reflux especially after large meals and on lying down at night. Notes regurgitation of sour-tasting fluid into the throat. Denies dysphagia. Denies weight loss or hematemesis.

She has tried over-the-counter antacids with partial relief. No prior endoscopy.

Vitals: BP 118/76, HR 74, BMI 26.4

Upper GI Endoscopy:
- Mild erythema of the distal esophagus
- No Barrett's esophagus
- Hiatal hernia (small, 2cm)

Assessment: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) with mild esophagitis.

Plan: Start PPI therapy (Omeprazole 20mg daily), dietary modifications, elevate head of bed. Follow up in 6 weeks.
